Jira/Agile Basics


Jira/Agile basics is an intensive half day training seminar designed to give development teams and stakeholders the knowledge they need to take full advantage ot the tool's many features. Learn More

A comprehensive introduction to the Jira Agile tracking and management tool, the de-facto standard support software for development efforts using the Scrum framework.

The Scrum Framework is revolutionizing the software development industry, providing productivity and quality improvements of 70% or more over traditional project management techniques.

The Scrum framework uses a number of artifacts for keeping track of both user requirements and development tasks. Jira/Agile is the de-facto standard tool used to manage the key Scrum “Product Backlog” and “Sprint Backlog” items.

Jira/Agile basics is an intensive half day training seminar designed to give development teams and stakeholders the knowledge they need to take full advantage ot the tool's many features.

It includes both informative lectures and hands on exercises to give students both knowledge and experience in using the tool.

Jira.Agile Basics is taught by internationally recognized Scrum Framework and Jira expert Frederik M. Fowler. Mr. Fowler is one of only about 50 individuals in the United States who holds the prestigious “Professional Scrum Expert” (PSE) certification. Mr. Fowler provides training and consulting services to help organizations adopt the Scrum Framework effectively and successfully.


JIRA/AGILE BASICS COURSE OUTLINE


What You Will Learn:

  • Creating and managing Jira/Agile projects
  • Creating and tracking Jira issues
  • Using a Jira/Agile Scrum Board.
  • Creating “burn down” charts
  • Giving users access to Jira/Agile Scrum Boards andProjects.

Class Content:

  1. What is Jira/Agile?
  2. How to get access to Jira/Agile
  3. Jira Structure
  4. Creatng a Jira Project
  5. Creating and tracking issues
  6. Creating a Scrum product Backlog
  7. Creating a Scrum Sprint Backlog
  8. Starting a Sprint.
  9. Assigning tasks and logging work
  10. Adding columns to the work view.
  11. Giving people access to the project.
  12. Class summary
